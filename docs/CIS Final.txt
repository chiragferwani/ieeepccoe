1. Aims
1.1 Primary Ai
The primary aim of this six-day summer school is to equip the next generation of researchers and practitioners with both the theoretical foundations and practical tools required to build Fine-Tuning Large Language Models and Inference Optimization (FTIO) systems for deployment in critical real-world applications. As AI models become increasingly embedded in healthcare, autonomous systems, finance, law, and public infrastructure, the urgent need for transparency, fairness, robustness, and interpretability has emerged as a global priority  reinforced by regulatory frameworks such as the EU AI Act (2024) and India's National AI Strategy.

This program is structured as a six-day residential and hybrid learning experience. It differs from standard short-duration summer schools by providing deep, sustained engagement with each topic area allowing participants to move from foundational understanding through practical implementation to independent capstone project work over a meaningful period of time.

1.2 Why This Topic Matters Now
Traditional black-box deep learning models, while powerful, face severe adoption barriers in high-stakes domains due to their lack of interpretability and trustworthiness. Regulators, institutions, and end-users are increasingly demanding that AI systems be able to explain their decisions in human-understandable terms. In India specifically, the deployment of AI in public services  healthcare diagnostics, judicial risk assessment, agricultural advisory, and credit scoring  makes the need for explainability not merely academic but a matter of public interest.

This summer school bridges the gap by teaching participants how to design Computational Intelligence (CI) systems that are inherently interpretable using fuzzy logic, rule-based systems, and transparent neural architectures and how to apply post-hoc explainability techniques to complex models that are already deployed. By combining classical CI strengths with modern deep learning, participants will learn to build systems that are both high-performing and accountable.

1.3 Problem Statement
Despite rapid advancements in AI research and deployment across India, there is a significant gap in structured, hands-on training for building fine-tuned and inference-optimized LLM systems particularly in emerging economies. Most academic programs continue to focus heavily on model accuracy and performance metrics, with limited emphasis on interpretability, fairness, adversarial robustness, and real-world accountability. This gap is further compounded by a shortage of experienced instructors in XAI and a lack of freely accessible, high-quality educational resources in regional languages or locally relevant application domains.

A six-day program, rather than a typical three-to-five day sprint, directly addresses this problem. It allows participants to engage deeply with each conceptual area, complete meaningful lab exercises, receive peer and mentor feedback, and produce portfolio-quality capstone work that demonstrates real competence to future employers and collaborators.

1.4 Topics Covered
Foundations of LLM Fine-Tuning
• Transformer architecture review and pretraining objectives
• Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) on domain-specific datasets
• LoRA, QLoRA, adapters, prefix tuning, and prompt tuning
• Instruction tuning and chat-style fine-tuning with FLAN and Alpaca
Alignment and Safety Fine-Tuning
• RLHF: reward modelling, PPO, and preference datasets
• Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) and ORPO
• Constitutional AI and red-teaming for safe deployment
• Evaluation: BLEU, ROUGE, perplexity, and human preference benchmarks
Inference Optimization
• Quantization: post-training quantization (PTQ), GPTQ, AWQ, and GGUF
• Pruning: structured and unstructured pruning for LLMs
• Knowledge distillation: training smaller student models from large teachers
• Speculative decoding, continuous batching, and PagedAttention
Production Serving and Deployment
• vLLM, TGI, and TensorRT-LLM: high-throughput serving frameworks
• Cloud deployment: AWS SageMaker, GCP Vertex AI, and Azure ML for LLMs
• RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) integration with fine-tuned models
• From Fine-Tuning to Inference: Designing Scalable LLM Systems with RAG and Graph-Based Retrieval
• Cost and latency optimization: token budgets, caching, and batching strategies

1.5 Expected Outcomes
By the end of the six-day program, participants will be able to:
• Fine-tune open-source LLMs (LLaMA, Mistral, Phi) using LoRA/QLoRA on domain-specific datasets
• Apply quantization (GPTQ, AWQ) and pruning to reduce model size and inference latency
• Set up and benchmark LLM serving with vLLM and TGI for production workloads
• Build an end-to-end fine-tuning and deployment pipeline from data preparation to serving
• Produce a documented capstone project: a fine-tuned LLM with optimized inference for a real application
• Engage critically with the engineering trade-offs and costs of LLM fine-tuning and deployment

3. Delivery Modes and Material Collection Plan
3.1 Overall Approach
The six-day format allows for a genuinely multi-modal blended learning experience that short programs cannot offer. Rather than compressing everything into lectures, the structure deliberately alternates between foundational instruction, applied lab work, peer review, and independent project time, a rhythm that reflects how practitioners actually develop expertise.

3.2 Pre-Program Online Preparation (Two Weeks Before June 22)
• Foundational video modules (15-20 minutes each) on prerequisites: transformer architectures, Python programming, PyTorch basics, and linear algebra
• Interactive quizzes embedded in the Learning Management System (LMS) to assess baseline knowledge and identify gaps before arrival
• Curated reading list of five to seven key papers per topic area distributed two weeks in advance
• Optional orientation session via video call for participants travelling from outside Pune
3.3 In-Person Daily Structure (Days 1-5)
Each of the first five days follows a consistent daily rhythm to help participants build routine and manage cognitive load across a sustained program.

TimeMonday/WednesdayTuesday/ThursdayFriday8:30-10:30Plenary or Guest LectureLecture (Theory)Lab Review / Q&A10:30-11:00BreakBreakBreak11:00-13:00Lecture (Theory & Methods)Fine-Tuning Clinic (small groups)Guest Industry Talk13:00-14:00LunchLunchLunch14:00-16:00Hands-on Lab SessionLab Continued / Pair WorkPoster Session / Peer Review16:00-16:30BreakBreakBreak16:30-18:00Discussion / Case StudyTeam Project WorkDay Reflection & Planning
3.4 Day 6: Capstone and Presentations
The final week is reserved entirely for capstone project completion, peer review, and formal presentations. No new instructional content is introduced. Each team of three to four participants presents a 20-minute talk followed by a 10-minute Q&A. External evaluators from industry and academia will assess projects against published rubrics covering technical quality, clarity of explanation, and practical applicability.

3.5 Fine-Tuning Clinics
A distinctive feature of this program is the daily Fine-Tuning Clinic  a two-hour small-group workshop (four to five participants per group) where teams debug and explain real black-box models using Python libraries including HuggingFace PEFT, TRL, bitsandbytes, and vLLM. Each clinic is facilitated by a lab instructor and targets a different model type or application domain each day. This format builds problem-solving competence in a way that traditional lectures cannot.

3.6 Hybrid Participation
All plenary talks will be live-streamed with real-time Q&A via chat for participants unable to attend in person. Recordings will be made available to registered participants within 48 hours. Lab sessions are in-person only to preserve the quality of hands-on learning, but online participants may follow asynchronous lab tracks with instructor support via the discussion forum.

3.7 Post-Program Continuous Learning (Six Months After June 28)
• A dedicated Slack and Discord channel will remain active for six months post-program
• Volunteer lecturers will provide written feedback on capstone projects submitted within three months
• Monthly virtual seminars on emerging LLM and inference optimization topics will be organized for alumni
• Participants will be encouraged to submit their capstone work to IEEE CIS student competitions or LLM workshop tracks

3.8 Material Collection and Dissemination
Video Recordings
• All plenary talks, lectures, and panel discussions will be recorded in HD (1080p) with high-quality audio
• Recordings will be edited to include synchronized slides, speaker video, and closed captions for accessibility
• Final edited recordings will be uploaded to the IEEE CIS Resource Center within three months of the program
Comprehensive Course Materials
• Lecture slides in PDF format with full speaker notes for all thirty-plus sessions
• Jupyter notebooks fully documented for all hands-on lab sessions (minimum 24 notebooks across six days)
• Curated bibliography of key papers and tutorials for each topic area
• Weekly problem sets with model solutions for self-assessment
Digital Handbook of LLM Fine-Tuning and Inference Optimization
• A 150-200 page compiled handbook integrating lecture notes, annotated code examples, and domain case studies
• Published as an open-access resource under a Creative Commons CC-BY 4.0 license
• Distributed in print form to all in-person participants and in digital form globally via the IEEE CIS Resource Center

6. Target Audience
This summer school is designed to serve participants across four groups. The six-day format is particularly well-suited to students who can commit a full portion of their summer to intensive learning, and to early-career researchers who want structured, mentored project experience.

• Senior undergraduate students (final year) in Computer Science, Electronics, and Data Science seeking practical AI skills before entering the workforce or graduate programs
• Graduate students (Masters and PhD) working on AI/ML research who need structured exposure to interpretability and fairness methods
• Early-career researchers and postdoctoral fellows who want to add efficient and deployable AI techniques to their existing skills and develop a portfolio project
• Industry practitioners from companies deploying AI in regulated or high-stakes domains  healthcare, finance, legal, and infrastructure  who need to understand and implement XAI requirements

Prerequisites: Basic Python programming, familiarity with either PyTorch or TensorFlow, and a working understanding of neural networks. No prior experience on fine tuning of llms is required.

Expected cohort size: 60 in-person participants, with an additional 100+ online participants following the live-streamed plenary sessions.

7. Relevance to IEEE CIS
This summer school is centered on the rapidly evolving domain of fine-tuning Large Language Models (LLMs) and optimizing their inference for real-world applications.
• Advanced Fine-Tuning Techniques: The program covers state-of-the-art approaches for adapting pre-trained LLMs to specific tasks, including LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation), instruction tuning, Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), and alignment methods. Participants will gain hands-on experience in improving model performance while minimizing computational cost.
• Efficient Inference Optimization: A strong emphasis is placed on deploying LLMs efficiently, covering techniques such as quantization, pruning, knowledge distillation, and batching strategies. These methods ensure faster inference, reduced latency, and scalability across edge and cloud environments.
• Robust Evaluation and Reliability: The school introduces frameworks for evaluating LLM outputs, including confidence estimation, uncertainty handling, and robustness testing. Participants will explore how to ensure consistent and reliable performance in production systems.

The program aligns with the broader goal of advancing cutting-edge computational intelligence by focusing on LLM fine-tuning and inference optimization, two of the most critical and high-impact areas in modern AI. It provides participants with both theoretical foundations and practical skills required to build scalable and efficient AI systems.
Participants will be encouraged to contribute to leading research venues and apply their learning to real-world challenges, fostering innovation and strengthening the research ecosystem.
All materials will be submitted to the IEEE CIS Resource Center, and all activities will be registered through IEEE vTools. A dedicated IEEE Collabratec workspace will be established for participant collaboration. Pre-event learning modules will be uploaded to the IEEE Learning Network (ILN). IEEE CIS branding will appear on all certificates, banners, and digital content produced during and after the program.
